# Lab 3 (Workflow 2): Project Paper → Slides

**Goal:** Turn a project paper (or a long document) into a presentation
**slide outline** using NotebookLM Studio and Gemini.

> **Note:** Features in NotebookLM's **Studio** are updated frequently. Button
> names may differ slightly — look for the option equivalent to what is
> described.

---

## Part A — Get the structure from NotebookLM

1. Upload your project paper (or a public sample document) as a new source.

   > ⚠️ Use a document you are authorized to share; avoid confidential
   > information.

2. Open the **Studio** panel (usually on the right).

3. Generate a structured output to use as your outline, for example:
   - **Briefing doc** — a structured executive summary.
   - **Study guide** — a breakdown of key topics and questions.

   ![Structured output from Studio](img/03-studio-output.png)

4. Copy that structured output — this becomes the basis of your slides.

---

## Part B — Turn it into slides with Gemini

1. Go to `https://gemini.google.com`.

2. Paste the structure from Part A, and use a prompt such as:

   ```
   Berdasarkan ringkasan di bawah, jana rangka slaid pembentangan sebanyak
   8 slaid. Untuk setiap slaid, beri satu tajuk dan 3 bullet ringkas.
   Gunakan Bahasa Melayu.

   [tampal ringkasan di sini]
   ```

   ![Slide outline generated by Gemini](img/03-gemini-slides.png)

3. Refine with follow-up prompts, for example:
   - "Make slide 3 more concise."
   - "Suggest a closing slide with a summary and next steps."
   - "Suggest an engaging presentation title."

4. Copy the final slide outline into your presentation tool (PowerPoint,
   Google Slides) for further development.

---

## Expected outcome

A **slide outline** (8 slides: title + 3 bullets each) generated from your
project paper, ready to be transferred into a presentation tool.
